10 Brands. 1 System. 8 Sites.

Promoted from contractor to UI/UX Design Lead — managing digital engagement for 10 hair extension brands. Built a unified design system, established a research program, mentored an incoming designer, and led the launch of 8 brand websites.

Overview

Beauty Industry Group is a portfolio company managing 10 hair extension brands across direct-to-consumer and professional market segments. When I joined as a contractor, each brand was operating in isolation — no shared components, no unified research process. I was promoted to UI/UX Design Lead to change that.

Team

The Challenge

10 brands. No shared design language.

Each brand had a unique identity, customer base, and price point — from DTC consumers to professional stylists. Every project started from scratch because there were no shared components. No standardized research process meant design decisions were based on assumptions, not evidence.

Key Findings
1No shared components — every site redesign started from zero
2Design decisions based on assumptions, no consistent research process
3Team scaling with an incoming designer who needed onboarding and mentorship
410 brands with unique visual identities needed a system that could flex for each

How might we simplify the enrollment process so new distributors can complete it confidently
— on any device, without a mentor?

The Solution

Build the foundation first, then the brands.

The answer was a unified design system with responsive, reusable components that supported brand-specific workflows. Each brand could express its own visual identity while drawing from shared, production-tested foundations — eliminating redundant work across every project.

The Work

System • Research • Launch • Repeat

Every brand had unique needs, but the underlying UX problems were the same. I built the infrastructure once and made it work for all 10.

01 · Design System
One foundation. Ten expressions.
Built a design system with responsive, reusable components — buttons, cards, nav, PDP layouts — with brand-specific theming on shared foundations. More than doubled production speed.
02 · Research Program
Grounded in real behavior.
Created and ran regular focus group sessions connecting brand stakeholders with consumers. Insights fed each brand's long-term UX roadmap — replacing guesswork with real user data.
03 · Insights Framework
Insights that didn't stay siloed.
Developed a scalable framework for cultivating research across all 10 brands — so insights became a shared resource informing the entire customer journey, not one-off findings.
04 · Mentorship
Building the team as work scaled.
Onboarded and mentored an incoming UX designer — establishing design and project management best practices that kept quality consistent as the team and brand count grew.

Reflection

The hardest part wasn't building the system. It was getting 10 brands to trust it.

Each brand had its own stakeholders, opinions, and history. The design system only worked because I involved brand teams early — showing them how shared foundations actually gave them more control over their identity, not less. The research program had the same dynamic: people had to see the value before they'd participate. Once they did, it became the most requested resource across the company.
